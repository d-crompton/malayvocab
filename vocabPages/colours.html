Colours

When added after the name of a colour, Tua (meaning old) changes the meaning to the name of a "Dark" colour. For example, Hijau Tua means Dark Green.
When adding Muda (meaning Young) after the name of a colour, its meaning changes to a "Light" colour. For example, Biru Muda means Light Blue.
Although Tua and Muda can be added to any colour they are typically only added to Blue (Biru) or Green (Hijau).

For the names of other colours you may use Pekat (meaning concentrated) for darker colours and Cair (meaning dilute) for lighter colours.
For example, Coklat Pekat means Dark Brown and Ungu Cair means Light Purple.

To describe whether a colour is dark or light (for example when shopping for clothes of a specific colour), you use Gelap meaning dark and Terang or Cerah meaning lighter.

Do you have a colour that... Ada warna yang...
...is darker? ...lagi gelap tak?
...is lighter? ...lagi berang tak?

To describe a colour as -ish (i.e., reddish or greenish), you place ke- at the start of the word and -an at the end.
For example, in Malay Reddish is Kemerahan and Reddish Brown is Coklat Kemerahan.
