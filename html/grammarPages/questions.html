Asking Questions

In Malay, you can place questions words at either the beginning or at the end of a sentence. For example, "Why did he go?" can be either:

  • Kenapa dia pergi?
  • Dia pergi kenapa?

"What is that?" can be either:

  • Apa tu? (Tu is a shortened form of "Itu" meaning 'that')
  • Tu apa?

Tak

Tak is a shortened form of Tidak, meaning No. When Tak is placed at the end of a sentence it denotes that the sentence is a question. It is comparable to starting a question in English with "Do...?" or ending a question with "...or not?".

For example, "Do you sell toothpaste?" in Malay is Ada jual ubat gigi tak?

Ke and Kan

Ke and Kan can be placed at the end of a sentence to turn a statement into a question. These can be use useful as they signify that a quesiton is being asked regardless of the tone used.

For example, the statement Nak makan means "want to eat" but "Nak makan ke?" becomes the question "Do you want to eat?"

The difference between Ke and Kan is that Kan is used for questions where the asker expects the other person to agree with them. It is comparable to adding "...right?" to the end of a question is English. For example, "Nak makan kan?" can be translated as "You want to eat, right?".
