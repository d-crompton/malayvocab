Adjectives Grammar

The following are different grammatical rules regarding adjectives.
For the translation of specific adjective words in Malay, go to our Adjectives Vocabulary page.

Se- / Comparrison

When the prefix Se- is added to the front of an adjective, it changes its meaning to "As X as...". For example, Besar means Big and Sebesar means As big as....

The sentence Kucing ini sebesar rumah means This cat is as big as a house.

Sangat / Very and Too

Sangat, when placed before an adjective, means "very" (e.g., sangat mahal means "very expensive").
When placed after an adjective, sangat means "too" (e.g., mahal sangat is "too expensive").
