Positions and Prepositons

In his book Speak Malay!, Edward S King suggests the Malay words for top, underneath, front and back (atas, bawah, depan and belakang) should be treated as nouns rather than prepositions (i.e., atas meaning "the top" instead of "on top of").

Top Atas
Underneath Bawah
Back Belakang
Front Depan

Instead, in formal Malay, each of these 'nouns' should be used with one of the following prepositions - ke, di or dari (to, on/at or from)
The following are examples using the word for top - Atas:

Going to the top (when in motion) Pergi ke atas
At/On the top (when stationary) Di atas
Coming from the top Datang dari atas

In colloquial speech, the position 'nouns' are used without a preposition. A few examples include:

On top of the table Atas meja
Underneath the cupboard Bawah almari
Back of/behind the house Belakang rumah
In front of me Depan saya
