The prefix Se- may be used in place of satu (one). For example, instead of Satu Ratus - meaning one hundred - you can use Seratus.
Sepuluh and Sebelas require the use of the se- prefix.

Numeric Classifiers

Numerical Classifiers (Penjodoh Bilangan) are words added between the number of an object and the object noun to denote you are counting an amount.
Malay has different Numerical Classifiers for counting different types of objects.

Malay Word Literal Translation Usage Examples
Batang Trunk Used to count long, slim objects or sticks Tiga batang pensel - Three pencils
Biji Seed Used to count smaller objects. This includes when counting fruit (even though Buah means fruit). Enam biji epal - Six apples
Tujuh biji telur - Seven eggs
Buah Fruit Used to count larger objects. It is not used when counting fruits Dua buah meja - Two tables
Tiga buah kereta - Three cars
Empat buah rumah - Four houses
Buku Book Used to count types of book Empat buku latihan - Four exercise books
Ekor Tail Used to count animals Empat ekor anjing - Four dogs
Keping Piece, Sheet Used to count pieces of something, sheets of paper or documents Sekeping roti - A piece of bread
Sembilan keping kertas - Nine sheets of paper
Orang Person, People Used to count people Dua orang anak - Two children
Tiga orang putih - Three white people (westerners)
Pucuk A shoot of a plant Used to count letters (as in post) and firearms Lima pucuk surat - Five letters
Sepucuk senapang - A gun

When counting a single instance or object you add Se- prefix to the classifier (i.e., seorang, sebiji).
