Time

There are three words in Malay that mean "time". Each has a slightly different meaning:

Masa Time that is quantifiable (e.g., plenty of time, not enough time, a little time). In colloquial Malay, Masa and Waktu are used interchangeably. However, when in doubt use Masa.
Waktu Used for naming time (e.g., Waktu Makan is Meal Time and Waktu Kerja is Work Time)
Pukul Used for "o'clock" (e.g., Pukul Lima is "Five o'clock"). Literally translates to "to strike". Pukul is not interchangeable with Masa or Waktu is this context.

Masa can also be used to demonstrate "when X" or "while X". For example, "When I was in London" is Masa saya di London and "While I was looking at him" is Masa saya tengok dia.

Additional vocabulary:

Suku is used to mean "Quarter past". For example Lima Suku is Quarter past Five. 5:45, or Quarter to Six is Pukul Lima Tiga Suku which can be interpreted as three quarter pasts (3 x 15 = 45) - although this approach is not often used.

Another way to say Minutes to Hour (e.g., Quarter to Seven) is using the format Kurang [minutes to] pukul [next hour] . Quarter to Seven becomes Kurang suku pukul tujuh. Note that suku can be used instead of fifteen (Lima belas).
