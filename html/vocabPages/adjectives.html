Baharu and Baru / New

In formal Malay, Baharu means that something is "brand new" or the latest.
Whilst Baru is used within the context of time (i.e., lately, recently or just). For example Saya baru makan means "I just ate".

Tua and Lama / Old

Both Tua and Lama translate to "Old". But Tua is used to describe people and Lama is used to describe objects.
However, Lama can also be used to mean old as in "previous". For example, Kawan Tua is a friend who is elderly but Kawan Lama is someone you've known for a long time or someone who is no longer a friend.
